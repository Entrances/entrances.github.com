Story 2

1. Vanessa is sitting in the couch

Vanessa is sitting in the couch. Another glorious day in North America. I wonder what Mr. Fernsmith is doing, she thinks. Maybe I should call him. She crosses her elgs. Or maybe I should just relax. Feed that hedgehog in the garden. I haven't been feeding him milk for a while. Maybe I should check my tablet. They say a comet is ising and approaching Earth as we speak. Time for concern? No, probably not. She leans back. What a nice day, perfect weather as always here in the desert. The milk man always rings twice as they say. No need to worry and no need to fret. The comet can wait until another day. Maybe I should call Sarah. We could have a bachelorette party in my house. But that would inevitably attract the frat boys. The phone is ringing. No, it was only in my imagination. What is imagination anyway? Frank Davis once said imagination is a virtual state. Maybe he was correct. In the architecture of ancient Badagascar we can clearly see influence of that line of thought. I wish I could build a house, she thinks. Then I could live there with my friends. Make cucumber salad, watch the sunrise, swing in the lianas and stuff like that. What a woman has to go through these days. Anyway, she thinks and relaxes comfortably in the white couch, Peggy might be up to something interesting. Apparentl;y she is exploring the world on that icefreighter MS Vanilla. Also, I should return the petunias I borrowed from her last year.
